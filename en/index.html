Search for Lost Meaning
Today the history of mankind seems simple and straightforward. Civilizations, countries, wars. Flourishes, disasters, cataclysms. Faith, gods, sacred texts, naive philosophies. The twentieth century, progress, space, computers.
We think we are smart and educated and look down on our strange and naive ancestors. Cheops sent his daughter to a brothel to earn money for the pyramid. The Spartans threw weak children off a cliff. Socrates never wrote anything down. Diogenes lived in a barrel. Mithridates had a golden chariot. Nero burned Rome to build himself a palace. And there are thousands more of these stories we learn history from. And who really is naive?
All right, the past is too distant. But why did they give six Berber horses for Hamlet and six French swords for Laertes? Why did the oprichniks have brooms and dog heads? Why did Pushkin write fairy tales? Where in the looking glass did the forest of missing names come from? What is "Stalker" about?
We laugh at conspiracy theories, but we do not realize that reality is far more unbelievable than the most desperate fantasies.
In our project we will prove to you that the paradoxes of history, the mysteries of religions, the secrets of geniuses and the secrets of the human brain are all parts of a single puzzle. We will show you that it is necessary to doubt even the most obvious things. First and foremost in the obvious...
We have compiled the clues and evidence into the first part of the book “The Origin of Meaning”. The book is structured in the form of a quest. By telling the necessary facts before offering a ready-made solution, the book gives you the opportunity to break your head over the riddles yourself.
You can be among the first to get the book by supporting the project. 
If you can't wait to see the riddles in question, here's a video.
If you are not afraid to find the real Lukomorye, to understand Diogenes, to learn the mysteries of the Strugatskys, then our provocations are for you. These are not proofs, but an attempt to make you start doubting what seems absolutely obvious.
Are you ready for a serious conversation?  The "proofs" section is for you. There you will be able to understand the Cherubim and find the Ark of the Covenant. Yes, you will indeed "palpate" the very Ark of the Covenant and be convinced of its authenticity.
